# 👋 About Me

I’m a junior undergraduate (2023–2027) pursuing a B.Eng. in **Information Engineering** at **South China University of Technology (SCUT)**.

Currently, I’m a visiting research intern at the **Shenzhen Institutes of Advanced Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences (SIAT, CAS)**, where I’m extremely fortunate to be advised by **Dr. Wenqi Fang** and **PhD candidate Zhuoyu Wu**. My work focuses on **ultra-lightweight medical image segmentation**, exploring how minimal architectural overhead can improve segmentation performance for resource-constrained healthcare scenarios. It’s been a pleasure collaborating with the talented team here.

I’m planning to apply for **PhD programs starting Fall 2027**, and always open to casual chats, academic discussions, or potential collaborations. Feel free to reach out via [email](mailto:jingzhizhang569@gmail.com)!

# 🔬 Research Interests

I strive to build **scalable, energy-efficient, and hardware-friendly AI systems**. My current work focuses on **Parameter-Efficient Architecture Design**, creating ultra-lightweight modules for resource-constrained scenarios. Looking ahead, my roadmap is clear:

- **From Algorithms to Systems:** Transitioning from standalone model design to **Edge AI & On-device Deployment**. I plan to investigate the synergy between sparse architectures and edge-cloud pipelines, aiming for low-latency, HW-aware solutions.

- **Beyond Medical Imaging:** While medical AI is my current anchor, I am driven to extend efficient AI principles to **AI for Science** and general **Signal Processing** tasks, tackling diverse data modalities with minimal computational footprints.

- **Green AI Commitment:** Dedicated to **Green AI**, I seek to reduce the energy consumption of deep learning models through architectural innovation and efficient inference strategies.

# 📝 Selected Research

### [Gain-Aware Prediction-Space Recursive Controller]()

A plug-and-play 189-param module for polyp segmentation that tracks discrepancy and uncertainty to refine logits, delivering consistent gains across seven backbones without architectural changes. <span style="color:#8B0000;">**First author**</span> · Submitted to *ICONIP 2026*.

### [RIGS-Refiner: Risk-Guided Recursive Refinement]()

A 519-param plugin introducing risk-guided selective repair for colonoscopy segmentation, outperforming heavier post-processing methods by leveraging prediction-space priors. <span style="color:#8B0000;">**First author**</span> · Submitted to *APSIPA ASC 2026*.
